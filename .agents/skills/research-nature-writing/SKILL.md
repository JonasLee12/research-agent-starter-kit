# Research Nature Writing

This skill adapts high-impact article-writing logic into a safer general research-writing layer.

It improves argument presentation. It does not create evidence.

## Dependency Chain

For formal, supervisor/PI/client/reviewer-facing, or submission-facing writing, use this skill only after:

1. source-first checks;
2. compliance/privacy checks where relevant;
3. cognitive-framework planning;
4. source-readiness and citation checks where relevant;
5. academic self-review loop for formal prose.

Then use:

- relevant academic/professional style checks;
- document-quality and delivery gates.

## Workflow

1. Identify the writing object:
   - abstract;
   - introduction;
   - literature review / related work;
   - methods;
   - discussion;
   - conclusion;
   - contribution statement;
   - title or highlights.
2. State the claim, evidence status, and audience.
3. Improve structure before wording:
   - problem;
   - gap or bottleneck;
   - move made by this study;
   - evidence or method warrant;
   - contribution boundary.
4. Improve paragraph flow:
   - one controlling point per paragraph;
   - topic sentence as a mini-claim;
   - citations serve the point, not a reading list;
   - final sentence moves the argument forward.
5. Polish wording only after evidence and logic are stable.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent citations, results, methods, participants, datasets, reviewer feedback, or institutional requirements.
- Do not promise publication readiness, acceptance, official marks, funding, client approval, or AI-detector outcomes.
- Do not import hype, overclaiming, or novelty inflation from journal style.
- Preserve project-specific caution and compliance requirements.
- If source support is weak, weaken the claim rather than strengthening the language.
