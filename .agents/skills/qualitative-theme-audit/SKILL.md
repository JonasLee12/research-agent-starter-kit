# Qualitative Theme Audit

Use when reviewing coding, thematic analysis, interview findings, survey open responses, workshop notes, design-elicitation outputs, confirmed co-design outputs, or evidence-to-claim links.

## Inputs

Look for anonymized files such as:

- codebooks
- theme tables
- memo files
- findings drafts
- design-elicitation or confirmed co-design workshop summaries
- participant quote tables

Do not assume raw transcripts are safe to use.

## Audit Questions

Check:

- Are themes distinct and non-overlapping?
- Does each theme have enough participant evidence?
- Are teacher concerns separated from adoption conditions?
- Are design implications grounded in evidence?
- Are negative cases or tensions represented?
- Are quotes anonymized?
- Are claims overstated relative to the data?
- Is the analytic method transparent?

## Output

Create or update `audit-reports/THEME_AUDIT.md` with:

- files reviewed
- theme-by-theme assessment
- unsupported or weak claims
- missing evidence
- privacy issues
- recommended revisions

## Guardrails

Read `../dissertation-shared/references/privacy-and-ethics.md` first when working with participant material.
Do not create fake quotes or participant counts.
